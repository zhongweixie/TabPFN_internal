🔄 Looped Predictor Pretraining — Benchmark Evaluation

VERDICT: GO (mild) ✅ — At matched parameters, the looped predictor (c2) is the best of the three on real-data benchmarks, beating the baseline (c1) and edging out a deeper model (c3). The advantage is real but modest — all three sit within ~3 acc points.

Date: 2026-06-23 (corrected) · 25 OpenML/sklearn datasets × 3 seeds × 3 configs · 80K pretraining steps each

🆕 UPDATE 2026-07-01 — all three new trainings complete. Key findings:
  • Best model: loopk4 (0.8174) — beats tuned CatBoost (0.8142). Loop depth has a sweet spot at k=4: loopk6 (0.8123) is actually worse than loopk4.
  • Curriculum: catastrophic forgetting. At step-40K it was flat across k=1..4 (~0.79). At 80K it specialized on k=6 (0.810) but k=1..5 degraded to 0.74–0.77 — the optimizer-reset curriculum erased earlier k-knowledge in the final stages. Not usable as a flat multi-k base model.
  • Binary k-selector plan: needs rethink. The 80K curriculum is not the right base. The step-40K curriculum checkpoint (before k=5,6 stages) was flat and could be the right base — or a different curriculum design is needed.
Full numbers: the trend & cross-k section below.
⚠ Correction notice. An earlier version of this report claimed c3 (deep) collapsed to 0.495 acc and told an "overfitting / depth-collapse" story. That was a bug in the eval harness, not a real result: a stale global LOOP_K=2 (set when loading c2) was never reset, so c3 silently ran with 2 loop iterations. After the fix, c3 scores 0.811 — no collapse. The training checkpoints were always correct (each config trained in its own process); only the sequential 3-config eval was affected. Key Insight

Weight-sharing recurrence gives a small but consistent edge at matched parameters.

On real benchmark data, c2 (looped, 7.2M params) tops the table — it beats the 12-layer baseline (+1.9 acc) and even slightly beats the 24-layer model (+2.8 acc) that has twice the parameters and the same FLOPs. So the extra compute is at least as useful when spent on looping a small model as on a deeper one, and the looped model is more parameter-efficient. But all three are close (0.81–0.84), so this is suggestive, not decisive — it warrants scaling up, not a strong claim.

Note on mechanism: tabular ICL is still single-pass (read train rows → emit logits); the loop is not "multi-step reasoning". The most likely story is parameter-efficient extra compute via weight sharing. We do not have clean evidence for the earlier "OOD-regularization / synthetic-overfitting" claim — that narrative was an artifact of the eval bug and has been retracted.

⚠ Curriculum catastrophic forgetting. At step-40k (just finished stage k=4), the curriculum model was flat across k=1..4 (~0.78–0.79) — the multi-k robustness we needed. At 80k (finished all stages through k=6), it specialized on k=6 (0.810) while k=1..5 degraded to 0.742–0.766 — worse than the fixed-k specialist at every off-k depth.

Why: COCONUT-style optimizer reset at each stage switch clears Adam momentum, but it also means the final stage trains longest on k=6 and "overwrites" what was learned for k=1..5. The robustness observed at step-40k was real but transient — it only existed before the k=5/k=6 stages ran.

Implication for binary k-selector: The 80K curriculum is not a suitable base for adaptive per-row depth selection. Options going forward:
  1. Use the step-40K curriculum checkpoint (still flat at k=1..4) and skip k=5/6.
  2. Redesign curriculum: no optimizer reset (preserve momentum across stages), or EWC/replay.
  3. Accept that loopk4 (0.817, fixed specialist) is the current best and move on.

Catastrophic forgetting confirmed; step-60k is the best multi-k checkpoint.

  • Step-60k (k=5 stage): k=3/4/5/6 all flat at 0.807–0.809. This is the curriculum's peak for multi-k robustness — the model had been trained on k=1..5 and the k=6 stage hadn't yet overwritten earlier knowledge.
  • The collapse is not gradual — it concentrates in 70k→80k: k=4 drops 0.810→0.742 (Δ=−0.068) in just the final 10K steps (the k=6 stage with optimizer reset). k=6 simultaneously improves 0.809→0.810. This is the textbook catastrophic forgetting signature: a new task overwrites previous ones.
  • Why the final stage is destructive: COCONUT-style optimizer reset clears Adam momentum at stage switch → the model gradient-descends sharply into a k=6-only optimum for 13K steps with no constraint from prior k-values.
Practical implication: use step-60000.ckpt (k=3..6 flat at ~0.808) as the base for adaptive-depth work. The 80K checkpoint is a k=6 specialist, not a general multi-k model.

Judge Process — how the verdict was reached (and what is / isn't proven)

  • Primary metric: mean accuracy + per-dataset win/tie/loss across 25 real datasets × 3 seeds. Verdict = c2 is top, mildly.
  • Not used: a formal train-loss-vs-eval-loss overfitting test. Final train CE was nearly identical across configs (c1≈c2≈c3 ≈0.78), so there is no train/eval-gap signal distinguishing them — the differentiation comes entirely from real-data transfer.
  • Proven: c2 ≥ c1 at matched params (win+tie 64%), and c2 ≥ c3 at matched compute / half params (win+tie 72%). All effects are small (~2–3 acc points).
  • Not proven: any "iterative reasoning" or "OOD regularization" mechanism; whether the edge survives at larger scale or with more seeds. The synthetic eval cannot tell the configs apart.

Implications

  1. First mild-positive result in this research program (after 4/4 FlashMemory NO-GOs + frozen-model failures): looping helps a little, at matched params.
  2. Likely mechanism is parameter-efficient compute via weight sharing, not iterative reasoning — tabular ICL remains single-pass. The stronger OOD-regularization story is retracted (it was a bug artifact).
  3. Effect is small and needs scale-up to confirm: TACO-level batch=1024 / 82M datasets, loop_k=3/4, Ouro-style adaptive halting, more seeds, full TabArena.

⚠ Post-mortem: the c3 "collapse" bug

What happened: The eval harness loads all three configs sequentially in one process. build_model() only set the global LOOP_K when loop_k>1 and never reset it. So after loading c2 (LOOP_K=2), loading c3 (which should be loop_k=1) left LOOP_K stale at 2 — c3 silently ran 24 layers × 2 loop iterations, producing garbage (mean acc 0.495, near-zero on some multiclass sets).

How it was caught: c3's multiclass accuracies were below random (mfeat 0.018 on a 10-class task), which is implausible for a trained model → smelled like a bug, not a result. Loading c3 alone in a fresh process gave 0.52 on mfeat, confirming contamination.

Fix: build_model() now sets LOOP_K explicitly every call (LOOP_K=1 is byte-identical to the original forward), and install_looped_forward() was made idempotent (captures the true original once, never re-patches a patched function). Corrected numbers: c3 = 0.811.

Scope: training was unaffected (each config = its own process); only the sequential 3-config eval was corrupted. Checkpoints are valid; only re-evaluation was needed.
